Kentaro Miura

One of the most influential men in manga

A quick view into Kentaro Miura's carreer and life

  • Born in Chiba, Japan, in 1966
  • Created his first manga at the age of 10, in 1976, entitled as Miuranger
  • In highschool he significantly improved his artistical skills and started using professional drawing techniques
  • He published his first D≈çjinshi with the help of his friends in a magazine in 1982
  • At 18 years old he worked as an assistant for George Morikawa, creator of the famous Hajime no Ippo series. Morikawa quickly recognized Miura's high artistic talent and dismissed him, claiming that there was nothing he could teach him that he didn't knew already
  • He was admitted to Nihon University in 1985 after he submitted his project Futanabi which later would win him the prize of 'best new artist' on Shounen Weekly Magazine
  • In 1989 Miura started the serialization of Berserk on Monthly Animal House from Hakusensha Magazine
  • It was only in 1992 that Miura's fame grew after the launch of "The Golden Age" Berserk history arc, a manga where soon after would become one of the best contemporary manga series
  • Kentaro Miura died at the age of 54 on May 6, 2021, due to an acute dissection, leaving behind a legacy of one of the best selling manga of all time, which inspired many artists and works in the anime, manga, film and videogame industries
